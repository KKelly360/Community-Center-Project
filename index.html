Community Center Project

Tailoring Development to Deliverables

A project for a new community center involves multiple deliverables, from the building itself to senior services, a community website and educational training programs. This project matches the development approach and delivery cadence to the unique characteristics of each deliverable. Explore the cards below to see how and why. The Physical Building

Well-defined, tangible structure with established construction processes.

Approach: Predictive

Cadence: Single Delivery

Why this approach?

Scope is clear and stable. Detailed upfront planning (blueprints, engineering specifications) is essential. Changes are costly and disruptive.

Process Flow

Define Scope & Requirements (plans, permits)
Plan (schedule, budget, resources)
Execute (construction phases)
Monitor & Control (against baselines)
Close/Deliver (inspection and handover)

Relationship

The predictive approach supports a single delivery cadence because the deliverable (building) is best developed through sequential, planned phases leading to one final output.

Senior Services Program

Service-based program where needs may evolve with community feedback.

Approach: Iterative

Cadence: Multiple Deliveries

Why this approach?

Allows for launching individual services, gathering feedback, and refining or adding new services based on learning. Manages uncertainty about specific service demands.

Process Flow (Example)

Iteration 1: Deploy "Meals on Wheels"
Gather Feedback on effectiveness & demand
Adapt/Learn to refine the service
Iteration 2: Deploy "Transportation Service"
Continue cycle for subsequent services...

Relationship

The iterative approach enables multiple deliveries by focusing on developing, launching, and refining one deliverable (a service) at a time, with each cycle improving the overall program.

Community Website

Digital product where user needs and content requirements will continuously evolve.

Approach: Adaptive

Cadence: Periodic Deliveries

Why this approach?

Allows an initial launch with core features, followed by continuous updates, feature additions, and design refinements based on user feedback and analytics.

Process Flow (Example)

Sprint 1: Deploy basic informational site
Gather User Feedback & Analytics
Sprint 2: Add Services Section & Events Calendar
Prioritize backlog for next sprint
Continue with periodic updates...

Relationship

An adaptive approach supports periodic deliveries by breaking down the deliverable (website) into manageable updates and feature releases, allowing for continuous evolution.

CAP Training Program

Educational program that can be broken down into distinct, self-contained modules.

Approach: Incremental

Cadence: Multiple Deliveries

Why this approach?

Allows for the development and delivery of complete, usable training modules one by one. The full program is built by adding these functional pieces together.

Process Flow

Design overall training program structure
Increment 1: Develop & Deliver "Basic Training"
Increment 2: Develop & Deliver "Logistics Training"
Increment 3: Develop & Deliver "Patrol Training"
Integrate all modules into the final program

Relationship

The incremental approach facilitates multiple deliveries by focusing on building and delivering complete, functional parts (modules) of the overall deliverable (training program) sequentially.
